Investigation Overview¶

In this investigation I aim to find out the most correlated variables with diabetes outcome in patients.

Dataset Overview¶

This dataset is originally from the National Institute of Diabetes and Digestive and Kidney Diseases, version was downloaded from Kaggle.

The datasets consists of several medical predictor variables and one target variable, Outcome. Predictor variables includes the number of pregnancies the patient has had, their BMI, insulin level, age, and so on.

1. Distribution of Diabetes Status¶

34.9% of participants have diabetes while 65.1% do not have diabetes.

2. Are people with higher BMI more susceptible to diabetes?¶

  • Those without diabetes had a median bmi of about 30 and an IQR range of 25-35
  • While those with diabetes had a median bmi of about 35 and an IQR range of about 31-39

3. What is the relationship between the age and Diabetes Status of the participants?¶

  • Most people with diabetes were older than those without diabetes,
  • Most diabetic participants are around 30 years and 40 years as indicated by the distribution in the violinplot, with a larger population around the 30 year mark
  • The median age of diabetic particpants around 36 years
  • The median age of nondiabetic participants is around 27 years

4. What influence does Glucose and Insulin levels on Daibetes?¶

OBSERVATION

  • People with diabetes have a higher glucose level than those without.

  • Diabetic participants had a higher insulin level than those without diabetes although there seems to be a lot of overlap between the two categories.

5.What is the Relationship between the Ages, BMI Categories and Diabetes Status of the Participants?¶

  • There's a significant difference in the ages of diabetic and non-diabetic participants,
  • There's no diabetic underweight participant.
  • There's a slight decrease in the age of diabetic participants as you move from healthy weight category down to the class-3-obesity category.
